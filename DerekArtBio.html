Derek Hobbs born and raised New London, CT. Is now a Madison, WI based artist. He lives with his wife and 3 beautiful children. He is always discovering, always experimenting and never settling. Derek is mostly a self-taught artist, though he did complete the first semester of the Graphic Design program at Madison College. His other education is very diverse and informs the way he works, he holds a Bachelor’s of Science from Ball State University in Natural Resources Studies and a Web Software Developer Associates of Applied Sciences from Madison College. The ideology of his work is underpinned by his love of God, nature and the environment and the human condition. Where some of his series such as his Alaskan Landscape and Endangered Animals he borders on realistic representation and a more crude realistic approach caring less about the details to probe more of the essence of his subject.

Working in acrylic, oil, digital, photography and mixed media he brings his ideas and emotions alive on canvas and wood panel. His influences are derived from many sources, such as the impressionists, abstract expressionists, surrealists, pop art, realists, post modernists, design, nature, his kids, music and the list goes on. He describes it as putting everything into a coffee or tea fueled blender, to tap his subconscious where effulgent creations manifest themselves into this world.


His artwork has been featured in collaborative art shows at Madison’s Art In, various local coffee shops and at the Alicia Ashman Library located in Middleton, WI. While living in Indiana, Derek displayed some of his work at local coffee shops and a local pottery shop called the Artist Within. While there he created a street art piece along the greenway. During his time in Fayetteville, NC he worked on several pieces that ended up being stolen, work here also included a small mural of a beach theme for a local Beach Bingo business. Growing up in CT, Derek worked under John DeNardis, an artist whom builds custom store front displays for such clients as F.A.O Schwarz in NYC. He also worked as a painter doing stencil work for the Garde Arts Theater renovation project in the 90’s. Derek’s work has been shown at the local Hygienic Art Gallery during their annual everyone is an artist show.
